Rules and Regulations

Check-in and check-out timings:

The GS Inn must have fixed check-in and check-out timings that should be clearly communicated to the guests during booking. The timings should be strictly adhered to, and guests should be informed of any additional charges for early check-in or late check-out.

Room occupancy:

The inn should have a limit on the number of guests allowed in each room. The occupancy limit should be clearly defined and communicated to guests during booking. The inn should not allow more guests in a room than its occupancy limit.

Smoking policy:

The inn should have a strict no-smoking policy within the premises. Signs stating this policy should be prominently displayed in all public areas.

Noise policy:

Guests should not be allowed to create excessive noise that disturbs other guests. Quiet hours should be implemented, and guests should be informed of these hours during check-in.

Security policy:

The inn should have a robust security policy to ensure the safety of its guests and their belongings. This could include 24-hours surveillance cameras, secure locks on all doors and windows, and a security guard at night.

Damage policy:

Guests should be held responsible for any damages caused to the inn's property during their stay. The cost of the damage should be borne by the guest and charged to their credit card.

Lost and found policy:

The inn should have a lost and found policy for any items left behind by guests. These items should be kept in a secure location and handed over to the guest upon their request.

Payment policy:

Guests should be required to pay for their stay upon check-in, and any additional charges should be settled upon check-out. The inn should accept multiple payment options, including cash, credit cards, and online payments.

Food and beverage policy:

The inn may choose to provide food and beverages to its guests. However, these items should be charged separately and not included in the room rates.

Guest conduct:

Guests should be expected to conduct themselves in a manner that does not disrupt the peace and safety of other guests. Any behavior that is deemed inappropriate or offensive may result in the guest being asked to leave the premises without a refund.

By implementing these rules and regulations, the GS Inn can ensure a pleasant and safe stay for its guests while also protecting its own interests.
